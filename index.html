FUN FACTS ABOUT BIKES

The term “motorcycle” (or “motorrad” in German) was first used in 1894 by Hildebrand & Wolfmüller

Forbes magazine founder, Malcolm Forbes owned 50 Harley-Davidson motorcycles

Helmet use was actually mandated following Colonel T.E. Lawrence‘s (Lawrence of Arabia)
death in a motorcycle accident. He was killed after he swerved to avoid two boys riding a bicycle.

The world’s longest motorcycle was built in Gujarat, India in 2015 by Bharat Sinh Parmar,
sitting at 86 ft 3 in (26.29 m) long.

The Isle of Mann TT is held on closed public roads and the current
lap record is just under
17 minutes with an average speed of 133.9 miles per hour

The longest distance riding a motorcycle in 24 hours is 2023.5 miles (3256.5 kilometers) and was
achieved by Matthew McKelvey aka “Bushy” at the Phakisa Freeway in Welkom, South Africa, on 8 October 2014.

The highest three months for motorcycle theft are July, August and September while the lowest are February, December, and March.
